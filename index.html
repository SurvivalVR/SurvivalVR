Learning Objective

The learning objective of our VR experience is to address the topic of common survival/medical misconceptions and their consequences when people are ill-informed. With so much technology and so many resources at our fingertips, it's easy to forget how much we don't understand about surviving real, life-threatening situations. Our VR experience puts the user through a nature hike in which their partner is put in immediate danger and/or is severly injured. We then give the user options to pick what they would do to help their partner, making sure to include options about many common misconceptions typical users have on the subject. Although in a way this can be comical, its also a very real fact that people are losing their "innate" survival skills that early humans once had. With this basic knowledge slowly fading, more and more people are put in medical danger from other people mis-handling situations. This VR experience, in a way, is trying to demonstrate to the user how ready they are for these situations in a less stressful environment and demonstrating the future consequences of their choices.

User Choices/Decisions

These are the different choices that the user is faced with throughout the experience.

  • Choice 1: Mountain or Valley
    • Mountain Camping
    • Valley Camping
    The different locations will dictate which animal that you will encounter. Typically, bears will live high up in the mountains and snakes will tend to dwell by water sources, especially if they are water snakes, such as water mocosins.
  • Choice 2: How to react in the emergency situation
    • Valley/Snake Emergency
      • Splint
        • Using a splint to restrict movement is the best way to go. You want to wrap the area in a compression bandage as firmly as you would a sprained ankle. Do not cover the bite area.
      • Suck Out Venom (Popular Belief)
        • Trying to suck the venom out of a wound is generally not a good idea. It can damage the area around or contaminate the wound more and is not an effective means to help the victim.
      • Tourniquet
        • Using a tourniquet is typically not a good idea in most cases. After being applied for even 2 hours, permanent tissue damage can occur. They are also not effective in stopping the spread of venom as it is already in the bloodstream.
      • Avenge Your friend
        • Stupid. But,at least you got the snake back. (Believe it or not, people do this quite often)
    • Mountain/Bear Emergency
      • Loud Noise
        • Making loud noises and trying to scare off the bear is the best idea when it is violent. The idea is to make yourself seem as threatening as possible to make the bear go away.
      • Run
        • Running away from the bear is not a good idea. Bears can run twice as fast as humans. By running from the bear, it sees you as less threatening and will chase you.
      • Climb
        • Climbing a tree would not be a good idea when trying to escape from a black bear. They are also able to climb trees, probably better than you.
      • Throw things at it
        • Throwing rocks at a bear to trying to scare it off is the a good idea when the bear is violent. The idea is to make yourself seem as threatening as possible to make the bear go away.

Our Reason For Picking This Topic
  • Choices aren't always obvious
    • With many topics based on an accelerated futures, the right decisions seem almost too obvious. Our first project idea, and the one we originally submitted, had to do with energy conservation in the household. While this is a serious and important topic to our future, it doesn't make for an exciting and beneficial VR experience. With the topic that we chose, survival, the correct answers are less than obvious, and the outcomes more stressful and personally taxing. It also gives the user an idea about themselves, and how prepared they would be in a highly stressful emergency situation, and gives them the outcomes of their hard decisions. Although your partner is not actually a real person, the experience, we hope, tends toward empathy of those around you and the decisions you make are not just about yourself, but another person involved.
  • This is a real issue
    • As far as social topics go, this certainly is one that isn't talked about too often, but it certainly should be. A lot of people today, especially young people who grew up with lots of technology, believe they are invincible because if they need to know something, they can just look it up on Google, right? But what happens when your phone is all but useless and you are left without a clue? Would we really know how to respond to life or death situations? Sadly, I think the answer is no. Now, luckily, things such as these don't happen too often, but if they did, it is important for people to understand how prepared they really are.
